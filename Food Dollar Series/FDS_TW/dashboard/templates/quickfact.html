{% blocktrans %} The Food Dollar Series provides an overview of the food system, with estimates of the farm share and of the distribution of food dollar, value added shares over time:{% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} For a dollar representative of all domestically produced food spent in the United States in 2021, U.S. farmers sold 14.5 cents of farm products to nonfarm establishments (farm share), down from 15.5 cents in 2020.{% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Farm production’s proportion of the food dollar decreased to 7.4 cents in 2021. This is the lowest value in the series so far, which has been reached in 3 prior years: 1993, 2002, and 2019.{% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Food-service costs per food dollar increased from 30.1 cents in 2020 to 33.6 cents in 2021. The foodservice share is again at a series high, after increasing for 8 straight years (2012–2019) and sharply decreasing in 2020.{% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Property income’s share of the food dollar increased in 2021 to 39.4 cents, after increasing 4.3 cents in 2020. This pre-tax income paid to the owners of non-labor factors of production is 4.2 cents higher than its historical average.{% endblocktrans %}

For calendar year 2016, the farm share was 24.0% of each food New Taiwan dollar expenditure and the marketing bill was 76.0% accounting for the remainder.

For calendar year 2016, farm production value added was 15.6% of each food dollar expenditure, implying that 8.4% from farm commodity sales (from the farm share 24.0%) was used to purchase products from the other industry groups. The 2016 industry group, value added food dollar also indicates about 37.9% of the food dollar value cover the services from food retailers (17.7%) and foodservice establishments (20.1%). Energy used throughout the food supply chain accounted for 3.9% of every 2016 food dollar expenditure. Advertising accounted for 1.2% of a food dollar expenditure.

For calendar year 2016, the primary factor series indicates 46.6% of every food dollar expenditure go to the compensation of domestic workers, 26.0% is dispensed as operating surplus, and the remainder is split among consumption of fixed capital, Taiwan government (Net taxes) and international assets (imports).
